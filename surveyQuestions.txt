### Survey: Market validation for **Family Continuity System** (digital light + postal mail release)

Below are **10 validated survey questions** designed to measure awareness, behaviour, willingness to pay, trust, and product fit for your initial product: a simple digital registry (account numbers, key info only) that triggers a **postal mail** of the stored details after verified death. Each question includes **answer options**, **question type**, and a short note on what the response reveals.

---

#### 1. How do you currently store important financial details (bank account numbers, policy numbers, demat IDs)?  
**Type:** Single-choice  
- I keep physical papers in a home file or locker  
- I store details in a digital note (phone notes, Google Keep, etc.)  
- I use spreadsheets (Excel / Google Sheets)  
- I rely on memory / tell a family member verbally  
- I don’t store them anywhere / I’m not sure

**Why:** Measures baseline behaviour and friction for adoption.

---

#### 2. Who in your family currently knows where your financial account details are kept?  
**Type:** Single-choice  
- Spouse/partner only  
- Spouse + one or more adult children  
- Sibling or parent  
- A trusted friend or advisor (CA/financial advisor)  
- No one / I keep it private

**Why:** Reveals visibility and risk of information invisibility.

---

#### 3. How concerned are you that your family would struggle to access your financial accounts if you died suddenly?  
**Type:** 5‑point Likert (Not concerned — Very concerned)  
- 1 — Not concerned  
- 2 — Slightly concerned  
- 3 — Moderately concerned  
- 4 — Concerned  
- 5 — Very concerned

**Why:** Measures emotional urgency and potential conversion drivers.

---

#### 4. If a secure service could store only account numbers and key identifiers (no passwords, no documents) and send a verified postal letter to your nominated contact after your death, how likely would you be to use it?  
**Type:** 5‑point Likert (Very unlikely — Very likely)  
- 1 — Very unlikely  
- 2 — Unlikely  
- 3 — Neutral  
- 4 — Likely  
- 5 — Very likely

**Why:** Tests core product-market fit for the light digital + postal model.

---

#### 5. Which verification method would make you most comfortable for triggering the postal release after death? (choose one)  
**Type:** Single-choice (security preference)  
- Death certificate + family request verified by team  
- Hospital / municipal death registry verification (automated)  
- Trusted contact confirmation + waiting period (e.g., 30 days)  
- Court/legal heir certificate required  
- I don’t trust any automated release; prefer manual family access

**Why:** Informs operational design and fraud mitigation.

---

#### 6. How much would you be willing to pay annually for the basic service (store account numbers + postal release) for one family?  
**Type:** Single-choice (price sensitivity)  
- Free / I would not pay  
- ₹199–₹499 per year  
- ₹500–₹999 per year  
- ₹1,000–₹1,999 per year  
- ₹2,000+ per year

**Why:** Directly measures price elasticity for the MVP subscription.

---

#### 7. If you needed help after a death (filing claims, forms, contacting banks), how much would you pay per claim for a basic concierge service (phone/WhatsApp assistance to file one claim)?  
**Type:** Single-choice (service willingness to pay)  
- I would not pay; I’d do it myself  
- ₹500–₹1,999 per claim  
- ₹2,000–₹4,999 per claim  
- ₹5,000–₹9,999 per claim  
- ₹10,000+ per claim

**Why:** Tests demand and pricing for the human assistance layer.

---

#### 8. Which of the following would increase your trust in such a service? (select up to 3)  
**Type:** Multiple-choice (max 3)  
- Government or bank partnership / endorsement  
- Physical postal mail confirmation (paper)  
- Local office or phone support in my city  
- Clear privacy policy and no-document promise  
- Insurance or escrow for misuse / fraud protection  
- Testimonials from other families / case studies

**Why:** Prioritizes trust-building features for marketing and ops.

---

#### 9. How would you prefer to discover and sign up for this service? (select up to 2)  
**Type:** Multiple-choice (max 2)  
- Through employer / HR benefits  
- Through my bank or insurer  
- Online ads / social media (YouTube, LinkedIn)  
- Recommendation from CA / lawyer / financial advisor  
- At hospitals / funeral service partners  
- Word of mouth / family referral

**Why:** Guides GTM channel prioritization and pilot partners.

---

#### 10. Demographics and context (short set) — please select the option that applies.  
**Type:** Multi-part (three short items)  
- Age group: 18–30; 31–45; 46–60; 61+  
- Primary occupation: Salaried employee; Self-employed / business owner; Retired; Homemaker; Other  
- Do you have dependents (children/elderly) who rely on your income? Yes / No

**Why:** Enables segmentation of responses and pricing by cohort.

## Survey design notes and recommended metrics

- **Survey length & format:** Keep it short (10 questions). Use mobile-friendly form (Google Forms / Typeform). Include a one‑line intro: *“We are testing a simple family continuity service: store account numbers only; we mail a verified paper to your nominated contact after death. This survey takes 3–4 minutes.”*  
- **Sample size:** For initial validation, target **300–500 respondents** across metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad). For statistically meaningful segmentation by cohort, aim for **1,000 respondents**.  
- **Target quotas:** 40% urban salaried, 20% small business owners, 15% NRIs (surveyed via diaspora channels), 25% older adults (45+).  
- **Key metrics to track:**  
  - **Interest score:** % answering 4–5 on Q4 (likely/very likely).  
  - **Willingness to pay:** distribution from Q6 and Q7.  
  - **Trust drivers:** top 3 choices from Q8.  
  - **Discovery channels:** top channels from Q9.  
  - **Visibility gap:** % answering “No one / I keep it private” in Q2.  
- **Open feedback:** Add an optional free-text field at the end: *“Any concerns or suggestions about a service that mails account details to your family after your death?”* This yields qualitative objections and language to use in marketing.
