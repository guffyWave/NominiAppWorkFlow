## **1. Un-claimed Funds in Indian Financial Systems — Data & Statistics**

### **Banks**

* As of **June 30, 2025, unclaimed deposits (dormant accounts or maturities not claimed for more than 10 years) stood at approx. ₹67,000 crore** in Indian banks. This reflects large amounts of savings and term deposits where account activity ceased and the money was transferred to the Reserve Bank of India’s *Depositor Education and Awareness Fund (DEA Fund)*. ([ETBFSI.com][1])
* Public sector banks account for ~87 % of the total, with **State Bank of India (SBI)** alone holding ~₹19,000 crore. ([The Indian Express][2])
* Recent parliamentary data suggests that unclaimed bank deposits transferred to RBI funds may have crossed **₹72,000 crore** by early 2026. ([The Times of India][3])

### **Insurance**

* Insurance companies are required to transfer unclaimed insurance amounts older than 10 years to the *Senior Citizens’ Welfare Fund*. The IRDAI reports **over ₹21,000 crore** has been transferred by insurers in recent years. ([www.ndtv.com][4])

### **EPF / Provident Funds**

* The Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) has many *inoperative* accounts — accounts with no contributions for long durations — with *unclaimed* amounts. A pilot initiative expects to credit over **₹10,900 crore** back to subscribers. ([The Times of India][5])

### **Mutual Funds & Markets**

* Unclaimed mutual fund redemptions and dividends have risen to around **₹3,400 crore** in 2024-25. ([The Economic Times][6])

### **Other Digital Data**

* Broader estimates (unofficial discussions) suggest total unclaimed financial assets across all categories (bank deposits, insurance, EPF, mutual funds, shares/dividends) could reach **₹1–1.8 lakh crore** or more. ([Reddit][7])

## **3. Common Problems Families Face Post-Death of Breadwinner**

### **Information & Access**

* Families struggle to *locate* all accounts, passwords, and physical documents.
* Even with nominees, they often **don’t know where assets exist or how to access them**.
* Digital accounts (online banking, email, investment dashboards) may be inaccessible without credentials. ([SAGE Journals][11])

### **Legal & Administrative**

* Legal heirs must sometimes obtain *death certificates, probate orders, and other documentation* before banks/institutions release funds.
* Assets may be *dormant or transferred to government funds* if no claim is made within statutory periods. ([ETBFSI.com][1])

### **Financial Strain**

* Immediate *expenses* — funeral cost, loan repayments, daily living — pressure families when income stops.
* Families may lack *budget planning or understanding of asset liquidity*.

### **Tax & Compliance**

* Inheritance tax, estate duties, and compliance documentation can be complex and vary by jurisdiction.

## **4. Specific Problems in Financial Context Post-Death**

### **Access to Funds**

* Bank and investment accounts may be dormant and treated as “unclaimed,” requiring special procedures to claim back. ([ETBFSI.com][1])
* EPF and pension (NPS) claims require thorough proof and often get delayed without proper record organization.

### **Insurance Claims**

* Life insurance payouts must be claimed; sometimes documents are incomplete, causing delays.

### **Debt Obligations**

* Outstanding loans (personal, home, credit cards) continue even after death; family may inherit responsibility depending on local law.

### **Loss of Knowledge**

* In many families, *only the deceased* knew where assets were held or how to access them.

## **6. Current Solutions and Practices Used**

### **Legal Instruments**

* **Wills & Trusts** — legal documents specifying asset distribution.
* **Power of Attorney** and **Letter of Instruction** — helping heirs understand where and how to access assets. ([Investopedia][13])

### **Estate Planning Services**

* Professional estate planners help assemble all financial details, designate beneficiaries, and store records securely.

### **Centralized Government Portals**

* *RBI’s UDGAM Portal* for unclaimed bank deposits — allows registration and search across banks to identify dormant funds. ([The Economic Times][14])

### **Nomination & Beneficiary Designations**

* Designating nominees in banks, insurance, EPF/NPS systems (Standard practice; however, families still need to know *what exists* and *where*).

### **Digital Tools**

* Password managers and secure document repositories help preserve critical credentials and records for executors.
